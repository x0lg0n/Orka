These three documents form the backbone of every client engagement. Understanding when and how to use each one is the difference between a smooth project and a messy one.

A proposal sells the work. A contract defines the work. An invoice collects payment for the work. They're connected — and in a modern agency workflow, they should flow into each other automatically.

This guide — coming soon — breaks down each document, when to use it, and how to connect them.

## Proposal vs Contract vs Invoice: The Relationship

- **Proposal** — Persuade the client to say yes. Send after discovery call.
- **Contract** — Legally define the engagement. Send after proposal acceptance.
- **Invoice** — Request payment. Send on milestone completion or delivery.

## Why They Should Be Connected

Each disconnected handoff creates friction. The client re-enters information. Terms get lost. Payment delays pile up.

In ORKA, a proposal converts to a contract with one click. The contract generates milestones. Completed milestones trigger invoices. It's one flow — not three separate processes.
