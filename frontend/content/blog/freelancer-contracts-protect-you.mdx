Contracts are the difference between a freelancer who gets paid and one who chases payments. A well-written contract defines scope, sets expectations, and creates a legal framework that protects both you and your client.

Every freelancer should have these clauses in every contract: clear scope of work, revision limits, payment schedule, termination terms, and intellectual property transfer conditions. Without them, you're exposed.

## What a Strong Freelancer Contract Includes

- Scope of work with specific deliverables
- Revision limits (2 rounds, for example)
- Payment terms and milestone schedule
- IP transfer terms
- Cancellation and kill-fee clauses
- Dispute resolution process

## Why Most Freelancers Skip Contracts

Fear of slowing down the sale. Or not knowing what to include. Both are fixable.

A one-page contract sent with a proposal signals professionalism. Clients who push back on contracts are often the same clients who push back on payments.
