## Introduction

Trust is the foundation of every client relationship. But trust alone doesn't get you paid.

Freelancers and agencies have relied on "pay after delivery" for decades. It works — until it doesn't. A client delays payment. A dispute arises over revisions. A project stalls mid-way and you've already delivered 60% of the work for 0% of the payment.

Escrow solves this. It's not a trust replacement — it's a trust enabler. Both sides commit funds upfront and release them as milestones are approved.

## The Trust Problem in Client Payments

The traditional payment flow looks like this:

1. Sign contract
2. Do the work
3. Send invoice
4. Wait 30–90 days for payment

The imbalance is obvious. You deliver value first. The client pays later. Any disruption — budget changes, stakeholder feedback, internal delays — stretches your receivables further.

This dynamic strains relationships. When money is tight, small disagreements become big disputes. Escrow removes the tension by making payment automatic on approval.

## Why Traditional Milestone Payments Fall Short

Many agencies already use milestone payments. A client pays 50% upfront, 25% at midpoint, 25% on delivery.

The problem? That first 50% is gone before any work is done. If the project changes direction or the client requests heavy revisions, you're negotiating from a weak position.

With escrow, funds are committed but not released until both sides agree the milestone is complete. This creates a natural approval gate that keeps projects aligned.

## Setting Up Smart Escrow Milestones

Effective milestone escrow follows a few rules:

1. **Define completion criteria clearly** — What counts as "done" for each milestone?
2. **Size milestones proportionally** — First milestone should be smaller (10–20%) to build trust
3. **Include a revision cap** — Define how many rounds are included per milestone
4. **Set a review window** — Client has N days to approve or request changes

<Callout variant="best-practice">
Keep milestones small enough that failure of one doesn't derail the whole project. A $10k project split into 5 milestones is safer than 2 milestones of $5k each.
</Callout>

## Is Escrow Right for Every Project?

Escrow works best for:

- Fixed-price projects with defined scope
- Milestone-based engagements
- New clients without payment history
- High-value projects ($2k+)

It may be overkill for:

- Hourly retainers with trusted long-term clients
- Very small projects (under $500)
- Equity or barter arrangements

## FAQ

### How is escrow different from an upfront deposit?

An upfront deposit is non-refundable and goes to the provider immediately. Escrow holds funds until milestone completion — both sides must approve before release.

### What happens if there's a dispute in escrow?

ORKA includes a structured dispute resolution process. Both sides present evidence, and funds are released according to the contract terms.

### Does escrow work for ongoing retainers?

Escrow is best suited for fixed-price milestone projects. For monthly retainers, a separate payment schedule may work better.
